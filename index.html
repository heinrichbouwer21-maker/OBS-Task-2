Kumba's new Ultra High Dense Media Separation project at Sishen Mine represents a transformational capital commitment. Yet its energy strategy has not kept pace. The result is a three-front exposure that threatens operational continuity, financial returns, and long-term ESG credibility.

SolarGravity — The Solution Concept

A Two-Phase Renewable Energy System Built on Sishen's Existing Infrastructure

SolarGravity combines an oversized solar field with pumped hydro storage, using Sishen's existing mining pits as rechargeable gravitational batteries — delivering dispatchable renewable power around the clock without chemical battery integration.

☀
Phase 01 · Daytime

Solar Generation & Energy Storage

An oversized 300 MW solar field generates more energy than the UHDMS plant requires. The surplus electricity powers pumps that move water from a lower dam to a higher-elevation reservoir — converting excess electrical energy into stored gravitational potential energy, ready to be dispatched on demand.

◎
Phase 02 · Nighttime / Low Solar

Hydro Dispatch & Continuous Power

When solar output drops, stored water in the upper reservoir is released through turbines, generating electricity that powers the UHDMS plant through the night. The water collects in the lower dam and is ready to be pumped uphill again the following day — a gravity-powered rechargeable battery.
